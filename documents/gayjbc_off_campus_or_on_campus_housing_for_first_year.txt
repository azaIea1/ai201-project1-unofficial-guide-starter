Title: Off campus or on campus housing for first year?

Post body:
Starting college in the fall. My school allows freshmen to live off campus if they want. My parents are pushing for on campus but I found an off campus apartment that's $400/month which sounds way cheaper. Is it worth it to live off campus as a freshman?

Comments:

- Strong recommendation: live on campus freshman year. The financial savings are real but you miss out on something hard to quantify — the spontaneous friendships, the community, the ease of meeting people that comes from being in the dorms. That stuff matters especially freshman year.

- $400/month might actually not be as cheap as it sounds once you add utilities, groceries, internet, transportation. On campus the sticker is higher but it covers a lot of things. Do the full comparison before deciding.

- I lived off campus freshman year to save money and regretted it. My close friends today are all people I met through dorm proximity — neighbors, people in my hall, friends of friends who visited. That network is much harder to build when you're off campus.

- The argument for off campus freshman year: if you already have friends going to the same school, if you struggle with dorm-style living, if you're very independent and the savings are meaningful for your financial situation. Those are real factors.

- Practically speaking: can you handle your own groceries, cooking, laundry, and all the adult stuff while also adjusting to college coursework and a new social environment? That's a lot of new things at once. For most 18-year-olds, dorm life provides useful scaffolding.

- At most schools the first semester GPA is heavily influenced by whether you're adjusting well. Having the dining hall, resident advisors, and other campus support services nearby matters more than people realize until they need them.

- I have friends who lived off campus from day one and did great. They were self-reliant people who didn't need the dorm experience to meet people. But they're the exception, not the rule.

- The commute factor shouldn't be underestimated. When you live on campus everything is 5 minutes away. Study session goes late? Walk back. Feel sick? Walk to the health center. Have a free hour? Go to the gym. Off campus you need to plan every trip.

- One financial reality: on-campus housing often packages room and meal plan together. If you're on financial aid, that combined cost might be partially covered. Off campus you're paying for both separately and aid may not cover housing as flexibly.

- My take: live on campus freshman year and use that year to figure out your friend group and get settled. Move off campus sophomore or junior year when you know what you want and who you want to live with.

- The $400/month apartment sounds amazing until you find out it has no heat included and is a 25-minute bus ride from campus with a bus that runs every 45 minutes. Look at the full picture of what you're getting.

- If your school is in a college town where off-campus housing is cheap and walkable and you have good friends already enrolled, the calculus changes. But generic advice for an 18-year-old starting college is: stay on campus your first year.
