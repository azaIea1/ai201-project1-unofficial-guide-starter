Title: How much cheaper is it to live off campus?

Post body:
Can someone actually do the math on this? I keep hearing "off campus is cheaper" but nobody gives concrete numbers. What does on campus vs off campus actually cost per year for a BU student?

Comments:

- I'll do the math for you. On campus junior year in a standard double in Mountainview: ~$7,800/semester for room and a required 150-block meal plan. That's roughly $15,600/year just for housing and a partial meal plan. Off campus I pay $575/month rent plus ~$90/month utilities plus ~$300/month food. That's $965/month or about $8,700 for a 9-month academic year. Savings: nearly $7,000 per year. That's real money.

- The numbers vary a lot by what on-campus option you pick and how many roommates you have off campus. Living alone off campus is actually pretty comparable to on-campus costs in some scenarios. In a 4BR split four ways is where the savings really show up.

- On-campus at BU you're looking at roughly $4,500-5,000/semester for a standard room plus a mandatory meal plan (which you may or may not use fully). Off campus the typical range is $450-700/person/month for a shared apartment or house, plus utilities, plus food.

- The meal plan comparison is important. Off campus I spend about $200-250/month on groceries and occasional eating out. The mandatory on-campus meal plan costs more than that even at the lowest required tier. If you're a light eater or cook at home, the meal plan is a bad deal.

- The "savings" from off campus also depend on your commute situation. If you need a car or rely on Ubers frequently, that adds cost. If you can walk or use the free campus shuttle, your savings are real.

- Don't forget the upfront costs of moving off campus: security deposit (usually one month's rent), first and sometimes last month's rent, buying furniture and kitchen stuff you didn't need in the dorms. Could be $1,500-2,000 all in just to get started. It's worth it but budget for it.

- I saved about $4,500 my junior year versus what I would have paid on campus. I put that toward paying down student loans which felt really good.

- The hidden on-campus cost people forget: mandatory fees, mandatory meal plan upgrades, room amenity fees. The sticker price isn't the real price. I've seen students pay $10,500/semester all in for on-campus. Off campus $500/month rent is $4,500 for 9 months.

- Utilities in Binghamton winter are no joke. Gas and electric for a poorly insulated apartment can run $150-200/month in winter. Still cheaper than campus but factor it in.

- One caveat: if you get a lot of financial aid that covers on-campus housing, do the math with your actual net cost not the sticker price. Some students get very favorable on-campus aid packages that make off campus less obviously cheaper.

- Per-person cost off campus depends hugely on how many roommates you have. 4 people splitting a house: $475-550/person. 2 people in a 2BR: $600-700/person. Living alone: $900+/month. The math works best with more roommates.

- I've never met anyone who did the full comparison and concluded on-campus was cheaper, unless they had unusual aid packages. The consensus in my friend group: off campus saves $3,000-7,000 per year depending on your setup.

- There are also lifestyle costs. You buy more household stuff (cleaning supplies, kitchen items, etc.) and sometimes spend more on food going out because you're tired from cooking. Ballpark it at $50-100/month in extra random expenses beyond rent and groceries.
