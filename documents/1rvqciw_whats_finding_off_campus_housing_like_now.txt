Title: Whats finding off campus housing like now?

Post body:
I'm a rising sophomore thinking about moving off campus next year. I know housing near BU used to be competitive — is it still like that? When should I start looking? Any advice from people who recently went through it?

Comments:

- It's still competitive, honestly more than people expect. The good places near campus on Vestal Parkway or Murray Hill go really fast. I started looking in September of my sophomore year and by the time I got serious in November most decent 4-bedrooms were already gone.

- Start in September or October if you want to have real options. I made the mistake of waiting until January and we ended up settling for a place that had mold issues and a landlord who never responded to maintenance requests. Not great.

- The timeline depends a lot on what you're looking for. If you want to be within walking distance of campus and pay under $600/person, you need to sign by November. After that the inventory gets picked over and you're left with overpriced or beat-up apartments.

- Completely agree with everyone saying to start early. My roommate group formed in August and we started touring in September. We signed our lease in October and it was one of the last good units at that complex. By December it was completely full.

- It's gotten slightly more competitive than it was pre-COVID in my opinion. Lots more students want off-campus housing because on-campus costs have gone up so much. The dorms are like $5,000 a semester with a mandatory meal plan — you can beat that pretty easily off campus even paying for food yourself.

- The area right off campus (like the blocks between Murray Hill and the Vestal Parkway commercial strip) fills up fastest. If you're okay being a bit further out toward downtown or in the Johnson City direction there's usually more availability into the spring, but you're looking at a longer commute.

- One thing nobody tells you: when you tour in September, a lot of the apartments you see are still occupied by current tenants. It can be hard to visualize because of the furniture and mess, but you still have to decide fast. Ask the landlord specifically about lease start dates and whether they'll do any repairs before move-in.

- We went with College Town Properties last year and they were reasonably professional about responding. Places around Leroy Street and Oak Street tend to be older but cheaper. Budget for utilities — our electric bill hit $200/month in winter because the insulation is bad.

- My advice: get your roommate group locked in before you start looking, not during. We lost two apartments because someone in our group kept being indecisive. Once you have 3-4 committed people you can move fast when you find something.

- Don't sign anything without reading the lease carefully. Some Binghamton-area landlords have clauses about who's responsible for lawn care, snow removal, or even pest control. I've seen leases that put all of that on tenants.

- Craigslist is still weirdly active for BU area rentals. Also check Zillow and Facebook Marketplace. Some of the better deals come from private landlords who don't use property management companies.

- The Mountainview complex is popular and fills up early but it's gotten pricey. Around $700-750/person now in a 3BR. You can find cheaper in a house-style rental on one of the side streets.
