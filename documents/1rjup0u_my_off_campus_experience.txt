Title: My Off Campus Experience

Post body:
Coming from on campus to off campus houses on the west side, I was pretty skeptical with renting on the west side. You hear horror stories of the neighborhood to be honest and it was a toss up between going to like a U Club (super overpriced tbh), or a nicer apartment downtown. I ended up touring a few spots with Stonebridge, Bearcats, and Amicus.

Stonebridge:
Price point was the upside. They were willing to undercut whoever. Downside: you get what you pay for with this company. The unit was just not it and I get people are living in the units so when you tour you expect like a lived in room. But I mean the outside looked like an abandoned lot. I just hear too many mixed things about this place. Like their website looks cool, and they have a bunch of places downtown, but also I heard from a friend still there they have a bunch of places condemned.

Bearcats:
I actually was pretty optimistic about this one because the site showed the unit photographed well and the price was competitive. HOWEVER, I soon found out through a friend that the housing was out of zone. Apparently you can't rent to students beyond certain streets and such. Honestly, I think I dodged a bullet. My friend this past year had a nightmare of a time getting his deposit back and they back charged him for utilities from the whole year. They couldn't even give him a real bill because the whole building splits the water and sewer so they just charged whatever. I think they ended up settling because my friend didn't want to fight them but also Bearcats management (who btw isn't like a local so he didn't know much anyways), knew they were in the wrong. The guy who worked with him lived in the city and didn't even know like about the utilities and stuff.

Amicus:
Their leasing guy like out of nowhere messaged me on insta (prob just like something they all do) and asked if I was looking for housing. Fairly certain I was kind of weirded out tbh but I looked them up and they were a legit company. I think we toured like 6 places some 1 and 2 beds and honestly I liked most of them. I chose to go with their place at 101 Murray. Downside: no laundry in unit. I am never home in general so like having to wait for my laundry kind of sucked but it wasn't like bad. The location is ok. I mean it's Binghamton. Closer to main is usually me BUT I never had a safety issue here. Free trash, parking (parked on the street mostly), and the unit itself was really nice. The inside is really nice, hard floors and granite or whatever counters and in the bathroom. It's not what I expected to be honest. The office staff were responsive. I know like it gets crazy in the beginning of the year with maintenance and stuff. Between Amicus and like two other landlords in this area I wouldn't go anywhere else. Another you get what you pay for in a good way.

Ultimately everyone has a different experience but mine was positive. I've seen some of the negatives on here but tbh I have never had an issue and still refer people to them. If I had the motivation to stay for grad school I would've stayed.

Comments:

- What are roommate red flags like first impressions especially when taking over someone's lease?

- Great writeup. Amicus has a solid reputation from what I've seen on here too. The Murray Hill area is generally the sweet spot for BU students who want something walkable and not totally sketchy.

- Bearcats splitting utilities across a whole building and then charging whatever is wild. That's basically just making up a number. Never renting from them.

- The U Club is so overpriced it's almost a joke. $800+/person for what? You can get a much nicer private place for $500-600.

- Good to know about Stonebridge having condemned units. That's a red flag that should be in a FAQ somewhere.

- Does Amicus do lease takeovers if someone needs to leave mid-year? That's been a pain point with other landlords.

- 101 Murray is a nice street. Close enough to campus to bike, quiet neighborhood. The lack of in-unit laundry is annoying but the coin laundry in the basement works fine.

- The Bearcats deposit situation is unfortunately common. Always make sure you get itemized receipts for any deduction from your security deposit. If they can't produce a real utility bill, you have legal grounds to dispute.
