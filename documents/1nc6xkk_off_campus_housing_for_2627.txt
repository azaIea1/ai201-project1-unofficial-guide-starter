Title: Off campus housing for 26-27

Post body:
Starting to think about housing for next year (2026-27 academic year). Junior year, probably 3-4 people. What's the market like and where should we be looking? Any landlords or complexes to avoid?

Comments:

- For the 26-27 cycle you should be looking right now. October and November are when most of the best units get snapped up. If you're asking in January you're already behind.

- Landlords to avoid: there are a few property management companies in the Vestal area with really bad reputations for ignoring maintenance and withholding security deposits. I won't name names in case I'm wrong but look up any address you're considering on the Binghamton area subreddit and ask specifically about the landlord.

- The Murray Hill area and the neighborhoods between Vestal Parkway and campus are ideal for walkability. Expect to pay $575-650/person for a decent 3BR in that zone. You can find cheaper but you'll usually be trading something — condition, distance, or landlord quality.

- I'm also looking for 26-27, in a group of four. We toured three places in the last two weeks. Our front-runner is a 4BR house on Leroy Street for $2,200/month ($550/person). Utilities not included. It's older but the landlord was responsive and the place was clean.

- Heat included vs. not makes a huge difference in your actual monthly cost. In a Binghamton winter an apartment where heat isn't included can run you an extra $80-120/person in gas. Always calculate total cost of occupancy, not just rent.

- Have you looked at Mountainview Apartments? It's popular with BU students and professionally managed. Probably $700-750/person now. More expensive but you deal with a real property management office rather than a private landlord.

- I'd prioritize a lease that starts August 1, not June 1. Some landlords try to charge you for June and July when you're not there. Unless you have a reason to be in Binghamton all summer, push for an August start.

- Check the lease for subletting policies. If your plans change and someone needs to leave, can they sublet their room? Some landlords prohibit it, which leaves you holding the financial bag if a roommate transfers or has an emergency.

- Parking: if any of your roommates have cars, check whether parking is included or extra. On-street parking near campus is limited and some streets have permit restrictions. We pay an extra $75/month for a parking spot but it's worth it versus the stress.

- Look at the square footage per person, not just the number of bedrooms. Some 4BR apartments are enormous houses and others are cramped 900 sq ft. Common space matters a lot when you're all home studying or hanging out.

- The Facebook group "BU Off Campus Housing" is legitimately useful. People post availability there, sometimes directly from landlords, sometimes from students subletting rooms mid-year.

- For landlord research: Google the property management company name plus "reviews" and also look at their BBB rating. It takes 10 minutes and can save you months of frustration.

- Don't forget about internet — BU's on-campus wifi is great but off campus you need to set up your own. Spectrum is usually the only realistic option in Vestal. Budget $60-80/month split among roommates.

- Try to see the unit in its actual condition, not a model unit if they show you one. Ask to see the specific apartment you'd be renting, including the bathroom and basement/laundry situation.
