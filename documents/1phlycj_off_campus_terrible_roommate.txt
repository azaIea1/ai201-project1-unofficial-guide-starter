Title: Off campus terrible roommate

Post body:
Living off campus this year with 3 other people and one of my roommates is making everything miserable. They never clean, they have their boyfriend over basically every night, they're loud until 3am, and they've missed their portion of the utilities twice. We're all on the same lease. Does anyone have experience dealing with this? Is there anything I can actually do?

Comments:

- This is one of the most common off-campus horror stories and unfortunately options are limited when you're all on the same lease. The landlord typically doesn't care about interpersonal conflicts — they just want the rent paid. Your leverage is mostly social.

- You need to have a direct conversation with this roommate, as uncomfortable as it is. Document everything — the times they're loud, the times they haven't paid, any damage. If it escalates to a legal issue later having documentation matters.

- Have you tried a roommate agreement? I know it sounds formal but sit down and write out shared expectations — cleaning schedule, quiet hours, guest policies, how utilities get split and when. If they violate something written down it's easier to hold them accountable.

- Been through something similar. The boyfriend situation is annoying but usually not lease-violating unless the lease has a guest policy. The utility non-payment is more serious — if you're covering their share and they don't pay you back that's a real problem. Keep records of every Venmo/Zelle request.

- If they keep missing utility payments and you end up covering it, you could potentially pursue small claims court for the money they owe you after the year is up. Keep every text, every payment request, every screenshot. It probably won't feel worth it but it's an option.

- One thing that helped us: we switched from splitting bills where one person pays and Venmoes others to everyone paying their share directly to the landlord if possible, or at minimum having a shared expense tracker that everyone can see. Transparency removes the "I forgot" excuse.

- Noise from 3am onward — if they're genuinely disturbing your sleep regularly that's a quality of life issue that affects your grades. I'd escalate past a direct conversation to a house meeting with everyone present. If three of you are bothered and one isn't, that's important information.

- Is the problematic roommate on the lease? If they are, you can't remove them without their agreement and the landlord's involvement. If they're subletting or their name isn't on the lease the situation is different — check.

- I dealt with a nightmare roommate situation junior year. Ultimately what worked was being extremely direct and specific: "You owe $85 from February utilities. You need to pay by Friday. If you don't I'm going to involve the landlord." Vague complaints got ignored. Specific demands with timelines got responses.

- The hardest part is that you signed a lease together expecting it to work. The best outcome now is usually a functional détente — not friendship but coexistence with clear rules. Some people just live differently and don't realize they're affecting others until someone tells them explicitly.

- Regarding the boyfriend: if he's essentially living there full-time, he's using utilities and taking up space that everyone is paying for. Most leases have a clause about extended guests (usually defined as more than 7-14 consecutive nights). Check your lease and if it's violated, you have something concrete to point to.

- After this year I highly recommend living with people you know very well or having serious pre-lease conversations about lifestyle. We made everyone answer questions about cleanliness, sleep schedule, noise tolerance, and guest expectations before agreeing to live together. Saved us a lot of drama.

- If things are truly unlivable, one option is to find someone to take over your lease if the landlord allows subletting. It's a pain to set up but sometimes the better option for your mental health.
